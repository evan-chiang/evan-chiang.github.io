Insight of the Land Subsidence in the Western Taiwan Coastal Area by Using PSInSAR Technique

Introduction

Land subsidence is a severe problem in western Taiwan. The modern geodetic data indicated the velocity of this area can up to 6.5cm/yr. Leveling and GPS can offer us accurate point data, but lack of planar data makes it hard to comprehend the land subsidence distribution. Thus, we can get the land subsidence distribution by using PSInSAR.

Procedure

Since PSInSAR might be distorted by several factors, we use GPS to constrain the velocity of the PSInSAR LOS result. After GPS fitting, removing antenna signal from GPS time series, we can constrain the PSInSAR data by convert GPS data into LOS direction. By doing this, the LOS velocity of ascending (VA) and descending (VD) can reveal truth ground displacement (LOS direction). Comclusion

SAR—different from GPS—can give us planar data, shows surface displacement instead of point displacement. This kind of information reveals surface displacement includes land subsidence and land uplift; moreover, due to data density, it can indicate fault location and help us to figure out new fault structures. By adding GPS data as a constraint, which can eliminate PSInSAR data distortion, making PSInSAR results more reliable.
